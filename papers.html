The Desperate Chase for Bonus Checks

Greed rules Wall Street, but how? This paper briefly investigates the motivating factor that led to the Great Financial Crisis, that being the enormous bonuses that executives and employees recieved for profits. As the risks associated with mortgage-backed securities (MBS) proliferated through the American derivatives market, so did the profits. Executives were compensated based on short-term profitability of their firms and quarter-specific performances, leading long-term risk tolerance and management to go down the drain.

March 31, 2024

From Wall Street to Beijing

This paper explores and compares the parallels between the 2008 Financial Crisis (Great Financial Crisis/GFC) and the modern Chinese economy, specifically the housing market, and makes suggestions for system reform. An abundance of government stimulus, relaxed lending practices, and shadow banking has resulted in the downturn of the Chinese property market and economy as a whole. This scenario is eerily familiar to the GFC, an era where the American federal government acted in similar manners, subsequently resulting in a violent explosion.
